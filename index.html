Position Guide
Why position is everything in poker
▶

Early Position (UTG, UTG+1) — You act first after the flop. Play only premium hands (top ~15%). You have no information about opponents' actions yet, so tighten up significantly.

Middle Position (MP, HJ) — You can widen slightly to ~20-25% of hands. You still have players behind who could 3-bet, but your range is stronger than late position callers expect.

Late Position (CO, BTN) — The most profitable seats. Open 30-45% of hands from CO, 45-60% from BTN. You'll have position postflop, letting you control pot size and bluff more effectively.

Blinds (SB, BB) — SB is the worst position—you're out of position the entire hand. BB gets a discount and defends wider vs opens, but still plays OOP. Defend BB vs BTN open with ~40-55% of hands.

Pot Odds & Equity
The math behind calling decisions
▶

Pot Odds = Amount to call ÷ (Pot + Amount to call). If you need to call $10 into a $30 pot, your pot odds are 10/(30+10) = 25%. You need at least 25% equity to break even on a call.

Quick Outs Math (Rule of 2 & 4)
Flop with 2 cards to come: Outs × 4 = approximate equity%
Turn with 1 card to come: Outs × 2 = approximate equity%

Common Draws
Flush draw: 9 outs (~36% flop, ~18% turn)
Open-ended straight: 8 outs (~32% flop, ~16% turn)
Gutshot: 4 outs (~16% flop, ~8% turn)
Two overcards: 6 outs (~24% flop, ~12% turn)

Bet Sizing
When and how much to bet
▶

Preflop Open Raises — Standard is 2.5x BB from most positions. Add 1BB per limper. From SB, use 3x since you're always OOP.

C-Bet (Continuation Bet)
Dry boards (K72r): 25-33% pot with high frequency
Wet boards (T98ss): 66-75% pot selectively
The drier the board, the smaller and more frequent your c-bet.

3-Bet Sizing — In position: ~3x the open. Out of position: ~3.5-4x the open. The goal is to build a pot where your range advantage translates to profit.

Overbet — Use 1.2-2x pot on polarized spots (rivers where you have the nuts or nothing). This maximizes value from strong hands and applies maximum pressure with bluffs.

Common Matchups
Preflop equity to memorize
▶
AA vs KK → 82% vs 18%
KK vs AKs → 66% vs 34%
QQ vs AKo → 57% vs 43%
JJ vs AKs → 54% vs 46%
TT vs AKo → 57% vs 43%
AKs vs QJs → 62% vs 38%
AKo vs 76s → 60% vs 40%
Key patterns: Pair vs overcards ≈ 55% favorite. Pair vs underpair ≈ 80%. Dominated hands (AK vs AQ) ≈ 73% favorite.
What is GTO?
Game Theory Optimal explained simply
▶

GTO = Unexploitable Strategy — A GTO strategy is one that cannot be beaten in the long run, regardless of what your opponent does. It's your "baseline" — the strategy you should use against unknown opponents.

Balanced Ranges — GTO requires betting with a mix of strong hands (value) and weak hands (bluffs) at specific ratios. On the river with a pot-sized bet, the ideal bluff-to-value ratio is about 1:2.

When to Deviate — Against players with clear tendencies (too tight, too loose, never folds, etc.), you should exploit those tendencies. GTO is your default; exploitation is your adaptation.

Indifference — In a GTO equilibrium, your opponent should be "indifferent" to calling or folding with their borderline hands. Your bet sizing and bluffing frequency are designed to make their EV identical regardless of choice.
